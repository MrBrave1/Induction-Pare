🛡The 8 Life-Saving Rules

⚠️ Violation of any Life-Saving Rule is a serious safety breach and may result in immediate removal from site.
Rule 1
📋 Permit to Work

Obtain a valid permit before starting any critical activity. Permits ensure risks are identified and managed.

Rule 2
⚡ Control Hazardous Energy

Isolate or control all hazardous energy sources (LOTO) before starting maintenance or commissioning work.

Rule 3
🧗 Work at Heights

Plan ahead. Ensure the area is safe, rescue plan is ready, and correct equipment is in place before working at altitude.

Rule 4
🏗 Lift Securely

Only trained persons assess loads. Restrict and isolate the lifting area and rig the load correctly before lifting.

Rule 5
🔧 Use Tools Correctly

Use tools and machines as intended. Inspect before use, maintain them well, and control the danger zone.

Rule 6
🧠 Be Ready for Action

Be physically and mentally fit. Never work under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or any impairing substance.

Rule 7
🚗 Drive Safely

Ensure all vehicles are in good condition. Drivers must follow all regulations. Only site vehicles enter the powerhouse.

Rule 8
🚪 Confined Spaces

Conduct atmospheric tests, have a qualified watchperson, prepare a rescue plan, and use correct equipment from the permit.

💡Why Do We Follow Safety?

Accidents carry two kinds of costs:

Direct Costs (Visible)

  • Compensation to injured/deceased workers
  • Medical treatment (first aid, hospital)
  • Repair or replacement of damaged equipment
  • Legal fines or penalties

Indirect Costs (Hidden – like the iceberg beneath the surface)

  • Lost production and project delays
  • Training and replacement of injured workers
  • Loss of company reputation and goodwill
  • Worker morale and motivation loss
  • Legal proceedings and administrative time

  • 🏗 Lifting Operations (with Lifting Plan)
